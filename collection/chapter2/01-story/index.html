The SF Nexus

The vast majority of twentieth-century literature remains undigitized, unavailable for access online even for the purposes of data analysis. The field of “distant reading” in literary criticism remains dependent upon impoverished datasets that are not representative of book history, especially mass-market genre fiction. We began a digitization project of mass-market science fiction at Temple University Libraries in the fall of 2017. We are currently expanding the project to include a range of special collections around the country with science fiction holdings…..



Author

Alex Wermer-Colan is a CLIR postdoctoral fellow at Temple University Libraries' Scholars Studio (formerly the Digital Scholarship Center). He has a Ph.D. in English and a specialization in digital humanities and text mining. He leads a digitization project of mass-market science fiction involving data curation for non-consumptive research. He advises and collaborates with faculty and students on text mining projects regularly. He uses text mining methods for analyzing the web as well as literature. His expertise has informed library practices for developed collections as data projects, improving cataloging and making discoverable our resources, and diversifying training of students in digital methods.
